About Jurassic Park

The Jurassic Park movie franchise, created by Steven Spielberg and based on Michael Crichton's novel, is a series of science fiction films centered on the resurrection of dinosaurs through genetic engineering. It explores the consequences of bringing extinct creatures back to life and humans' struggle to control them. The franchise is iconic for its groundbreaking visual effects, thrilling action, and exploration of humanity's ethical dilemmas in science and nature.

Jurassic Park (1993)

The groundbreaking first film introduces a dinosaur theme park where chaos ensues after the creatures break free.

The Lost World: Jurassic Park (1997)

Set on a second island, this sequel follows a rescue mission amidst a dinosaur outbreak.

Jurassic Park III (2001)

A group returns to the island for a rescue mission, facing new dinosaur threats.

Jurassic World (2015)

A new, fully operational dinosaur park faces catastrophe when a genetically engineered hybrid dinosaur escapes.

Jurassic World: Fallen Kingdom (2018)

Dinosaurs are rescued from an erupting volcano but end up being auctioned as weapons, leading to greater peril.

Jurassic World Dominion (2022)

The final chapter sees dinosaurs and humans coexisting, with global consequences as the creatures roam freely across Earth.
